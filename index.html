About Our School

High school 99 was formed in 1948 as the 5th male. And in 1954 it was merged with the girls' school. The school was located behind the main building of the Tajik State University. In 1976, the school was moved to a new building located on Lenin Avenue (now Rudaki Avenue), behind the Dushanbe Hotel, which until that time had been occupied by School No. 99. Currently, secondary school No. 21 is one of the few schools that teach in Russian. The school enrolls children from all parts of the city. Training is conducted in 2 shifts. The school also provides instruction in the state (Tajik) language for children from grades 1 to 4. They continue their education at other schools. At the moment, 2475 people study at the school. The school has 62 teachers. Of these, 58 people have higher education. Secondary special - 3 people. Unfinished higher education - 1 person.
